Game Design Principles

The Premise

Play a TTRPG about experiencing, exploring, and discovering the idyllic yet difficult world of Hylandia.

The Agenda

  • Portray a 'natural fantasy' world
  • Fill the characters lives with opportunities
  • Play to find out what happens

Game Leader Principles

  • Embrace the fantastic and unusual
  • Give every monster life
  • Name every person: who are they, what are they doing, and what do they want to be doing or what do they need help with?
  • Be a fan of the characters and their players
  • Don't fast-forward, find & reveal opportunities where you are at right now
  • Ask questions and use the answers
  • Begin with the fiction, end with inspiration (end with momentum and a path forward)

How-to

(when you don't know what to do, check if one of these helps)

  • Describe the situation (and re-describe as things change)
  • Follow the rules
  • Make moves
  • Exploit your prep

When to Make a Move

  • When everyone looks to you to find out what happens
  • When the players give you a golden opportunity
  • When they roll a failure (anything below the difficulty)

Frame: Give Opporutnities - These Make Adventure

(this aim sets the 'frame' for the moves named later on)

Healing: Something not whole
Experience: Learning not received
Discovery: Something novel or new
Exploration: Somewhere unintended
Challenge: Something undefeated
Mystery: Something not solved
Creation: Something asking to be expressed
Bonds: Relations in development
Rest: Time to settle, digest, and enjoy

Adventure Moves

  • Reveal a detail about the area: it's surroundings, weather, denizens, and activity
  • Show something damaged, and maybe why it's worth repairing
  • Offer a way to make something imperfect work well enough
  • Invite them to learn something new or a clear way to gain experience
  • Show them something worth investigating now or saving for later
  • Reveal something tempting and curious
  • Present an obstacle that needs handling or avoiding
  • Give them something incomplete, or not fully explained
  • Ask them to delve a little deeper, revealing something about their character or perspective
  • Introduce a new character or creature who is doing something and wants or needs something
  • Introduce a character with something to offer
  • Offer rest in an unlikely place
  • Poke a character with a frustration or annoyance
  • Queue a change in the environment
  • Give an opportunity that fits their capabilities
